Chapter 1 — What is Excel & Why It’s Used

Excel is a spreadsheet tool used for data storage, cleaning, analysis and visualization. It is fast, flexible, and ideal for small to medium datasets and quick analysis.

Chapter 2 — Excel Interface

Rows, columns, sheets, the ribbon, and formula bar. Know where to find functions and how to navigate multiple worksheets.

Chapter 3 — Data Entry & Formatting

Basic data types and formatting (number, date, text). Keep consistent formats and use built-in formatting options for currency and dates.

Chapter 4 — Sorting & Filtering

Sort by values or multiple columns, and use filters to focus on subsets of data.

Chapter 5 — Data Cleaning

Remove duplicates, handle blanks, and clean text using TRIM, CLEAN and other text functions.

Chapter 6 — Basic Formulas

SUM, AVERAGE, COUNT, MIN, MAX — use these for quick aggregations.

Chapter 7 — Logical Functions

IF, AND, OR — use to create conditional logic in cells.

Chapter 8 — Lookup Functions

VLOOKUP, HLOOKUP, XLOOKUP — essential for joining tables in Excel.

Chapter 9 — Text Functions

LEFT, RIGHT, MID, CONCAT — manipulate and combine strings.

Chapter 10 — Pivot Tables

Most important tool in Excel for summarizing data quickly. Group, aggregate and analyze without formulas.

Chapter 11 — Charts in Excel

Bar, line, and pie charts for visual summaries. Format axes, labels and legends for clarity.

Chapter 12 — Conditional Formatting

Highlight key values, use color scales, and create rules to flag important data.

Chapter 13 — Data Validation

Dropdowns and input controls to ensure data quality.

Chapter 14 — What-If Analysis

Scenario analysis and goal seek for exploring outcomes.

Chapter 15 — Excel Shortcuts

Keyboard shortcuts and workflow tips to speed up common tasks.

Chapter 16 — Build Excel Dashboard

Create small dashboards with pivot tables, charts and slicers to enable quick business insights.

Chapter 17 — Storytelling in Excel

How to structure a short narrative and present the answer clearly with supporting visuals.

Chapter 18 — Interview Preparation

Typical interview questions and short answers describing Excel usage, comparisons to SQL and Power BI.

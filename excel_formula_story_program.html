14-Phase Formula Roadmap

Order matters because analyst workflows build layer by layer.

Phase 1: Core Analytical Formulas

SUM, AVERAGE, MIN, MAX, COUNT, COUNTA, COUNTBLANK, ROUND, SUBTOTAL, AGGREGATE.

Scenario: Revenue totals and KPI summaries for reporting.

Phase 2: Logical and Decision Functions

IF, IFS, AND, OR, NOT, SWITCH, IFERROR, IFNA.

Scenario: KPI logic, pass/fail rules, error handling.

Phase 3: Text Analytics Functions

LEFT, RIGHT, MID, LEN, TRIM, CLEAN, CONCAT, TEXTJOIN, TEXTSPLIT, TEXTBEFORE, TEXTAFTER.

Scenario: Clean CRM exports and standardize names.

Phase 4: Date and Time Analysis

TODAY, NOW, DATE, YEAR, MONTH, DAY, WEEKDAY, WEEKNUM, EOMONTH, DATEDIF, NETWORKDAYS.

Scenario: SLA tracking, attendance, monthly reporting.

Phase 5: Lookup and Data Matching

XLOOKUP, VLOOKUP, HLOOKUP, LOOKUP, INDEX, MATCH, XMATCH, INDEX+MATCH.

Scenario: Join employee, product and revenue datasets.

Phase 6: Dynamic Array Functions

FILTER, SORT, SORTBY, UNIQUE, SEQUENCE, TAKE, DROP, CHOOSECOLS, VSTACK, HSTACK.

Scenario: Dynamic reports and live analytical sheets.

Phase 7: Conditional Analytics

SUMIF, SUMIFS, COUNTIF, COUNTIFS, AVERAGEIF, AVERAGEIFS.

Scenario: Region-wise sales and performance segmentation.

Phase 8: Error Handling and Data Quality

ISERROR, ISBLANK, ISNUMBER, ISTEXT, ISFORMULA, ERROR.TYPE.

Scenario: Error-proof dashboards and validation.

Phase 9: Advanced Reference Functions

OFFSET, INDIRECT, ADDRESS, ROW, COLUMN, ROWS, COLUMNS, CELL, HYPERLINK.

Scenario: Dynamic ranges and dashboard navigation.

Phase 10: Financial Functions

PMT, NPV, IRR, FV, PV, RATE.

Scenario: Loans, investments and financial planning.

Phase 11: Statistical Functions

MEDIAN, MODE, STDEV.P, STDEV.S, VAR.P, VAR.S, PERCENTILE, RANK, LARGE, SMALL, CORREL.

Scenario: Trend, ranking and correlation analysis.

Phase 12: Advanced Analytical Formulas

Nested formulas, multi-condition formulas, array formulas and optimization.

Phase 13: Dashboard Formula Engineering

Dynamic KPI cards, titles, filters and auto-updating reports.

Phase 14: Formula Optimization

Reduce calculation load, avoid volatile formulas, use structured references and named ranges.

How We’ll Teach Each Formula Chapter

1. Business Problem First

We begin with a company scenario, not syntax.

Example: Sales data is in one sheet and targets are in another. How do analysts combine them automatically?

2. Formula Concept

Then we explain what the formula does, why companies use it, and where analysts apply it.

3. Visual Excel Example

Every formula is shown with small tables, expected output, and a step-by-step breakdown.

4. Analyst Thinking

We compare formula choices, scalability, speed, and dashboard integration.

5. Practice and Challenges

Real analyst exercises, company-style tasks, mini assignments, and interview-style cases.

6. Dashboard Integration

You learn how formulas feed KPI cards, titles, filters, charts, and automated reports.
